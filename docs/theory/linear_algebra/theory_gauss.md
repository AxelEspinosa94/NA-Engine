# Gauss Elimination — Theory

Gauss elimination solves:

$$
Ax = b
$$

by transforming $A$ into an upper triangular matrix.

---

# 1. Steps

1. Pivot selection  
2. Row swapping  
3. Forward elimination  
4. Back substitution  

---

# 2. Requirements

- Matrix must be non‑singular  
- Pivot elements must be non‑zero  

---

# 3. Conceptual Example

$$
A = \begin{pmatrix}
4 & 3 \\
6 & 3
\end{pmatrix},
\quad
b = \begin{pmatrix}
10 \\
12
\end{pmatrix}
$$

Solution:

$$
x = (1, 2)
$$
