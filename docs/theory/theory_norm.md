# Matrix Norm — Theory

A matrix norm measures the “size” of a matrix.

The most common is the **2‑norm**:

\[
\|A\|_2 = \sigma_{\max}(A)
\]

where \( \sigma_{\max} \) is the largest singular value.

---

# 1. Properties

- \( \|A\| \ge 0 \)  
- \( \|kA\| = |k|\|A\| \)  
- \( \|A + B\| \le \|A\| + \|B\| \)  
- \( \|AB\| \le \|A\|\|B\| \)

---

# 2. Conceptual Example

For:

\[
A = \begin{pmatrix}
4 & 3 \\
6 & 3
\end{pmatrix}
\]

\[
\|A\|_2 \approx 8.544
\]
